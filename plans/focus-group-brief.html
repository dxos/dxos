Composer — Focus Group Brief · Draft, 2026-04-15

Composer Agent

A local-first workspace where AI agents read your real data — meetings, tasks, PRs, chat — and act across them. We want your gut reaction.

What we're asking of you

Watch a 3-minute demo video of a workspace that connects to your real tools (Trello, Granola, Slack, GitHub) and lets an AI agent reason across them. After watching, fill out the rating matrix and the five open-ended questions at the bottom. It should take you 10 minutes total.

Be direct. If something looks staged or gimmicky, say so. We'd rather hear "I didn't believe the Slack message was real" than polite applause.

Our thesis

Most AI agent tools today are cloud-hosted, stateless rule engines dressed up with an LLM. They fire webhooks, post messages, and forget everything between invocations. Composer takes a different shape:

  • Local-first. Your data graph (ECHO) runs on your laptop. Works offline, syncs when you're online, lives across your devices without a central server you have to trust.
  • Transparent agent. Every model call, tool call, and reasoning step is visible in real time in an Inspector panel. No black box.
  • Memory across your real data. The agent doesn't just process one webhook — it cross-references meeting notes, task status, PR history, and chat context when composing a response.
  • Plugins are source. Each connector is ~300 lines of TypeScript. You can read, modify, and write your own without leaving the repo.

Think of it as: what if Zapier, a notebook app, and an AI agent framework were the same thing, and all ran on your laptop?

What this is, and what it isn't

  • It isn't a product announcement. This is an open-source developer tool, shipping as a Show HN draft. No pricing, no waitlist.
  • It isn't a demo of finished software. The workspace runs in dev mode; you'll see a few "taking a long time to activate" warnings in the console during the recording. We're leaving those in — it's an honest look at the real thing, not a marketing reel.
  • It is a test of whether the story lands. Local-first + transparent agent + memory across real tools is the thesis. If you don't feel it after three minutes, we need to recut.
