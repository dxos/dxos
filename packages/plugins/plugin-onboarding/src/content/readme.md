# Welcome to Composer

Composer is a local-first workspace where documents, tables, diagrams, and AI agents all live together. It runs on DXOS — an open framework for building collaborative software that keeps your data on your device and works with or without a connection.

This document lives in your personal space. Edit it, delete it, or move it — it's yours.

## The building blocks

Everything in Composer is an **object**: a document, a sketch, a table, a spreadsheet, and more. Objects are provided by **plugins**, and you can add or remove plugins from the Settings dialog at any time. A few come installed by default:

- **Documents** — collaborative Markdown with inline comments.
- **Sketches** — an infinite canvas for diagrams and drawings.
- **Tables** — structured records you can sort and filter.
- **Sheets** — numbers, formulas, and charts.

You organize objects into **collections**, and collections into **spaces**.

### Spaces

A space is a self-contained workspace. It holds your objects and controls who can see them. Your spaces appear in the left sidebar; the one you're in now was created for you automatically.

### Collections

Collections group related objects together — think of them as folders that can nest to whatever depth you need.

## Working with agents

**Projects** let you collaborate with an AI agent on a goal. Describe what you want and the agent works alongside you inside the space, with full access to the objects you share with it.

```prompt
Create a project to build a CRM from my inbox.
```

## Collaborating with people

Spaces are real-time and multiplayer. Anyone you invite can work in the same document at the same time, and changes merge without clobbering each other.

To invite someone, select a space in the sidebar and use the **Share** button in the top menu to create a single- or multi-use invitation. The same button shows who currently has access.

## What Composer stands for

- **Private by default** — your data is stored on your device. No one sees it unless you invite them to a shared space.
- **Local-first** — Composer works offline and syncs when you reconnect. There's no central server holding your work hostage.
- **Collaborative** — real-time multiplayer is a core primitive, not an add-on.
- **Extensible** — need something we don't ship? Build a plugin for yourself, or share it with the community.
- **Open source** — DXOS and Composer are open source. The code is on [GitHub](https://github.com/dxos/dxos).

## Good to know

**No login, and no backups by default.** You didn't sign up because your identity lives on this device and browser — managed by HALO (the button in the lower-left corner). That also means if you lose this device, you lose your work. Add a second device from the HALO menu to keep a synced backup.
